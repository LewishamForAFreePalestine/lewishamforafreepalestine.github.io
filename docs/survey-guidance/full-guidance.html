Lewisham Pension Survey: Why Your Voice Matters

Looking for a quick summary? ← Back to TL;DR version

If you are a Lewisham pension holder, you should have received an email and letter from the Council asking you to complete a survey about how your pension is invested. You may find the email in your spam or junk folder.

This survey is happening because more than 3,000 local residents and pension holders have pushed for more responsible, transparent and financially safe pension investments. The Council is finally asking what you think. The survey closes on 6 December.

Your response genuinely matters.

What You Should Say in the Survey

These are the key steps if you want the Council to prioritise ethical, climate friendly and human rights based investment in the future:

  1. Complete the survey and encourage your colleagues to do the same.
  2. Ask for an ethical pension (Q10, fourth or fifth option).
  3. Select Human rights as your top concern (Q15 and Q20).
  4. In the written answers (Q14 and Q22), state that you want immediate divestment from companies complicit in the apartheid and genocide economy and a clear commitment not to invest in companies linked to human rights abuses.

These simple steps will ensure the Council understands what members want and expects.

Why This Survey Is Important

Lewisham's pension fund is part of the Local Government Pension Scheme (LGPS). The Council manages the fund on behalf of employees and pension holders and is legally required to protect members' financial interests and to consider environmental, social and governance (ESG) factors.

Fiduciary Duty: What UK Law and Global Guidance Say

Many people assume pensions must focus only on financial returns. In reality, UK law is clear that pension funds:

  • Can consider social and ethical issues
  • Should address human rights risks
  • Must avoid investments that could cause significant financial harm
  • May act on member views where there is no significant financial detriment
  • Should carry out human rights due diligence
  • Must consult scheme members on their investment strategy

This is supported by the UK Law Commission, Government guidance on Investment Strategy Statements and the United Nations.

Your view has legal relevance, which is why your survey response carries real weight.

Why This Matters Even If You Feel Neutral on Palestine

This campaign is not asking you to take a political position.

It is asking you to ensure your pension is:

  • Financially safe
  • Legally compliant
  • Not exposed to companies with clear human rights risks

Official UK Government Warnings

The Foreign and Commonwealth Office's Overseas Business Risk guidance for the occupied Palestinian territory warns that investing in companies operating in illegal settlements involves:

  • Legal risks
  • Financial risks
  • Reputational damage

The United Nations also instructs investors that involvement in settlement activity can amount to complicity in human rights abuses unless they can prove otherwise. Where harms cannot be prevented or mitigated, companies are expected to terminate their activities.

These principles apply directly to pension funds.

Where Lewisham's Pension Fund Stands Today

Lewisham currently holds at least 68 million pounds in companies that the United Nations identifies as directly linked to human rights violations in conflict areas, including the occupied Palestinian territory.

This is not only an ethical problem. It is a significant financial one.

Financial Risks Include

  • Sudden loss of value
  • Legal and compliance risks
  • Stakeholder and divestment pressure
  • Long term instability
  • Reputational damage

Ethical and responsible investments are generally more resilient and future proof.

Lewisham recognised this when it divested 800 million pounds from fossil fuels, following global best practice and the same logic used when investors withdrew from companies linked to South African apartheid.

What the Council's Decision Means for Your Pension

The Council has said that this survey will shape its next Investment Strategy Statement, which guides how more than 1.8 billion pounds of pension assets are managed.

Pension holders have the right to influence:

  • ESG policies
  • Responses to human rights risks
  • The long term safety and value of their pension money

However, many members noticed that the survey does not explicitly mention human rights, the arms trade or investments in occupied territories. This makes it especially important that you clearly state your views in the written sections.

Why Action Is Urgent

The Council has been considering this issue for more than a year while the international situation has worsened. During the past 24 months:

  • 1 in 33 Palestinians have been killed
  • 20,000 children have lost their lives
  • 1 in 14 people face life threatening injuries

These figures are recognised by international humanitarian bodies. Pension funds are expected to respond when human rights risks reach this level.
