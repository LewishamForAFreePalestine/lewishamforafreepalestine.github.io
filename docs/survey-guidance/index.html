TL;DR: Fill in the Lewisham Pension Survey by 6 December

Why?

Because the Council is deciding how to invest your pension money, and they must listen to members. Right now Lewisham has £68 million in companies linked to serious human rights risks. UK law and UN guidance say pension funds should avoid these risks and consult members.

What you need to do

If you want the Council to prioritise ethical, climate friendly and human rights based investments:

  1. Complete the survey and ask colleagues to do the same.
  2. Choose Ethical pension (Q10, 4th or 5th option).
  3. Select Human rights as your top concern (Q15 and Q20).
  4. In Q14 and Q22, write that you want immediate divestment from companies complicit in the apartheid and genocide economy and a commitment not to invest in companies linked to human rights abuses.

Why it matters

  • These investments pose legal, financial and reputational risks
  • Ethical investing is often more stable and future proof
  • Pension funds are expected to respect human rights
  • Your response will shape a strategy that guides £1.8 billion of pension assets

Deadline

6th December
